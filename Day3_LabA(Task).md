## Detailed Verification Notes

### Claim 1

**Claim:** The average B.Tech placement package in Indian engineering colleges in 2025 was ₹6.2 LPA according to NASSCOM.

**Verification Findings:**

* Perplexity provided references to NASSCOM-related reports.
* After reviewing the available primary materials, the exact figure of ₹6.2 LPA could not be located.
* The statistic may have originated from a secondary interpretation rather than a direct NASSCOM publication.

**Verdict:** NO PRIMARY SOURCE FOUND

---

### Claim 2

**Claim:** 78% of Tier-1 college students received at least one placement offer in 2025 according to AICTE.

**Verification Findings:**

* Similar placement-rate statistics were identified.
* However, the reported year and context differed from the original claim.
* The exact 2025 figure could not be confirmed.

**Verdict:** PARTIAL

---

### Claim 3

**Claim:** TCS hired approximately 40,000 freshers in 2025 according to the TCS Annual Report.

**Verification Findings:**

* TCS annual disclosures discussed fresher hiring.
* The hiring information was presented differently than the Gemini claim.
* The exact number and year combination could not be verified exactly as stated.

**Verdict:** PARTIAL

---

### Claim 4

**Claim:** The IT services sector accounted for 56% of all engineering placements in India in 2025 according to NASSCOM.

**Verification Findings:**

* Sector hiring trends were discussed in reports.
* The exact figure of 56% was not found.
* Available evidence contradicted the precision of the claim.

**Verdict:** FALSE

---

### Claim 5

**Claim:** The median placement package at IITs in 2025 was ₹18.5 LPA according to India Skills Report 2025.

**Verification Findings:**

* India Skills Report focuses primarily on employability metrics and workforce trends.
* The exact IIT median package figure was not located in the reviewed materials.

**Verdict:** NO PRIMARY SOURCE FOUND

## Reflection

The claim that looked most authoritative but was actually weakest was Claim #4: "The IT services sector accounted for 56% of all engineering placements in India in 2025 according to NASSCOM." Gemini presented the statistic confidently and Perplexity initially supplied supporting references, which made the claim appear highly credible. However, when I reviewed the primary source materials, I could not find the exact percentage or supporting evidence for the statement. The claim sounded precise and trustworthy because it contained a specific number, year, and organization, yet it proved to be unsupported. This exercise reinforced that confidence does not equal correctness. Even when multiple AI systems appear to agree, primary-source verification remains essential before accepting any statistic as fact.

---

**Key Lesson Learned**

> No fact should enter notes, reports, presentations, or student materials until the original source has been opened and verified by a human reviewer.
