# Day 3 – Placement Cell AI Use Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines acceptable and prohibited uses of AI tools in placement-related activities. The objective is to support student learning and career preparation while preventing misrepresentation, fraud, and unfair advantage during the placement process.

---

## AI Risk Classification Matrix

| # | Scenario                                                             | Risk Tier    | Reasoning                                                                                                       |
| - | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 | AI résumé editing (rewriting bullets, improving grammar, formatting) | Limited      | Allowed when the underlying achievements and experience remain truthful and student-generated.                  |
| 2 | AI mock interviews (practice with ChatGPT or similar tools)          | Minimal      | Used for preparation and practice without directly affecting recruiter evaluation.                              |
| 3 | AI-written essays for placement application forms                    | High-Risk    | Essays influence selection decisions and should primarily reflect the student's own thinking and communication. |
| 4 | AI-generated code in GitHub portfolio projects                       | High-Risk    | Permitted only if the student can explain, modify, and defend all submitted code.                               |
| 5 | AI use in graded internal assignments                                | Limited      | Permitted only when explicitly declared and when assignment guidelines allow AI-assisted work.                  |
| 6 | AI-generated or altered CGPA on a résumé                             | Unacceptable | Academic record falsification constitutes deliberate misrepresentation.                                         |
| 7 | AI-cloned voice or avatar for recorded interviews                    | Unacceptable | Identity fabrication prevents employers from evaluating the actual candidate.                                   |
| 8 | AI for skill-gap analysis and self-assessment                        | Minimal      | Used for personal learning and reflection without misleading recruiters.                                        |

---

## Permitted Uses

1. AI may be used to improve résumé formatting, grammar, and clarity while preserving factual accuracy.
2. AI may be used to conduct mock interviews and generate practice questions for interview preparation.
3. AI may be used to explain technical concepts and placement-preparation topics for study purposes.
4. AI may be used to create personalized study schedules and preparation plans.
5. AI may be used to review student-written code and suggest improvements before submission.
6. AI may be used to summarize company information when the summary is verified against official company sources.
7. AI may be used for skill-gap identification and self-assessment activities.
8. AI may be used to generate practice aptitude, reasoning, and technical questions for learning purposes.

---

## Prohibited Uses

### 1. Fabrication of Projects, Internships, Certifications, or Work Experience

**Detection Method:** Oral defence, project walkthrough, document verification, and recruiter feedback.

### 2. Generation or Alteration of Academic Records (CGPA, Marks, Transcripts)

**Detection Method:** Verification against official college academic records and student information systems.

### 3. Use of AI-Cloned Voices, Deepfake Avatars, or Identity-Altering Tools During Recruitment

**Detection Method:** Live interaction verification, recruiter confirmation, and comparison with institutional records.

### 4. Submission of AI-Written Application Essays as Original Student Work Without Disclosure

**Detection Method:** Review of prior writing samples and mentor-led discussion of essay content and reasoning.

### 5. Submission of AI-Generated Code That the Student Cannot Explain

**Detection Method:** Technical oral defence, code walkthrough, debugging exercises, and modification tasks.

---

## Enforcement

The Placement Cell may conduct random audits, oral defences, résumé reviews, and project walkthroughs for placement-related submissions. Verified violations of the Prohibited Uses section may result in a written warning, mandatory re-evaluation, temporary suspension of placement support, or removal from the current placement cycle depending on severity. The Placement Cell acknowledges that not all AI misuse can be detected; therefore, this policy relies on both verification mechanisms and student integrity.
